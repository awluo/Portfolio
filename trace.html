TRACE

A mobile app for contact tracing

Overview

My IS Milieux course term project was to design a Information Systems solution to help combat the COVID-19 pandemic. My proposed solution was to create a digital contact tracing tool with a mobile app. This could make contact tracing and notification instant upon case confirmation, acting much faster than the traditional manual method.The app gathers useful location and proximity data to inform potentially infected individuals and guide them towards the resources they need to prevent further spread of the virus.

Duration
Spring 2020 (2 weeks)

Team

Skills

Process

Background Research

Contact tracing helps control disease outbreak by tracking those who have been in contact with an infected individual to advise them to take protective measures and self-quarantine. This is a difficult process if done manually, requiring significant time and resources. In addition, human memory is not always reliable or accurate.

How It Works

The app works by broadcasting a unique Bluetooth identifier that other nearby phones can detect. Apps estimate distance between one another using Bluetooth signal strength. If less than 6ft of distance is sensed, they exchange identifiers and the contact is logged into the appâ€™s database. Thus, a temporary record of proximity events is created between individuals. Once an individual is diagnosed, the app alerts recent close contacts and prompts self-isolation.

Privacy Concerns

With adoption of contact tracing apps, there are ethical considerations to keep in mind:

Voluntary digital tracing (must opt-in) & Transparency: for what info is collected and how it will be used

Limit on how long data will be stored. Potential rotation of phone identifiers to lower risk of third-party tracking

Anonymized ids in databases, no disclosure of patient or user identity

Results

Final Design

After conducting background research, I created the following high fidelity wireframes for the contact tracing app.

  1. Users are prompted to login into the app and set their privacy preferences.
  2. They can set their primary contact information, so the app can can notify them if they have been in contact with a COVID-19 diagnosed individual recently. Once users receive the alert, they are redirected to a resources page, that provides information on next steps they should take (getting tested, self-isolating, etc.)
  3. There is also an accessible resources page for receiving updated and relevant local news about the virus.
